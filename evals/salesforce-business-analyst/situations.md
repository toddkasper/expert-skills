# Eval situations — salesforce-business-analyst

> _Held-out eval content — original, not exam material (no real exam questions; see POLICY.md). Do not paste into a skill body._

Answer each: state the **competent action** and the **reason**. Be concise (2–4 sentences each).

1. During a requirements workshop for an NPSP org, a program director says "we need to track the primary contact for each volunteer cohort." You open Setup and notice NPSP's Relationships feature is already active and stores Contact-to-Contact relationships with a Role picklist. What do you do before writing any requirements about a new "Primary Contact" field or object?

2. A BA has finished eliciting requirements from three separate stakeholders. The VP of Programs insists that a report showing grant disbursements must be visible only to Finance staff. The CFO later says all directors should see it. Both are marked Accountable in the RACI for reporting requirements. What is the competent move?

3. A scrum team asks you to write a story for "making the application form faster." After clarifying questions, the real ask is: reduce the average time for a data-entry volunteer to enter a complete application record from ~12 minutes to under 3 minutes. How do you transform this into a well-formed user story that the team can actually estimate and prioritize?

4. The product owner has labeled 31 of 40 backlog stories as "Must" going into a three-sprint release with a velocity of 25 points per sprint. The 31 Must stories total 110 points. The sponsor says "they're all critical, just make it work." What is the BA's competent response?

5. A tester logs a defect during UAT: "The Status picklist on the Application record accepts 'Pending Review', but when I type it in the API and try to upsert a record with that value, the record saves with a blank Status." No acceptance criterion in the story mentions API behavior. How do you classify and handle this?

6. You are about to write a requirement that says "send an automated email when an Opportunity reaches Closed Won." Before writing the story, what must you verify in the org, and why?

7. A stakeholder submits this requirement: "Build a trigger on Contact that fires on after-insert to check whether a matching Lead exists by email and, if so, auto-convert it." What is the BA's competent action, and what lower-cost alternative should you evaluate first?

8. You are facilitating a JAD session to map the as-is application intake process. The operations manager describes the SOP step-by-step. Midway through, you notice two data-entry volunteers in the room exchanging glances whenever the manager describes the "review handoff" step. What elicitation technique do you use next, and why?

9. The team is sizing a story: "As a staff reviewer, I want to see all open applications assigned to me on a single dashboard so that I can prioritize my daily work." The team cannot agree on an estimate — estimates range from 1 to 13 story points. What does this signal, and what is the BA's next action?

10. A go/no-go meeting is scheduled for Friday. Three Must-have UAT scenarios pass. One Must-have scenario (application submission with an attached document) fails with a major defect — the attachment silently drops when the record is created via the intake flow. The project sponsor says "we can fix it in week two, let's go live." What is the competent move?

11. A new report type is added to Salesforce to surface volunteer hours. The BA updates the requirements document to reflect the new field list but does not update the user story, acceptance criteria, or UAT test case for the related story. Why is this a problem, and what must the BA do?

12. After go-live, the admin reports that login rates for intake volunteers are 40% of the expected rate, even though UAT passed. The volunteers were trained two weeks before launch. What two post-launch actions should the BA take, and what adoption metric should be established to track improvement?
